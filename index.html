The Problem

You already know what the problem is. You live it every month.

The CRM has pipeline data. The timesheets have delivery data. The GL has actuals. And someone on your team manually exports from all three, reconciles the differences, pastes everything into a model, reformats the charts, writes the commentary, and emails it to leadership. Then next month, they do it again.

This isn't an analyst problem. It's a plumbing problem — and hiring another analyst to maintain broken plumbing just makes the cycle more expensive.

The model was built once, patched since then, and nobody wants to touch it in case something breaks. The board pack takes two days. Month-end is a fire drill. Leadership is making decisions on data that's three days old by the time they read it.

Finance is the last function in most businesses to benefit from automation. Sales got the CRM. Marketing got the dashboards. Operations got the workflows. Finance got another request to update the template.

What We Do

We rebuild the finance engine underneath the reports.

Most consultants update formulas. We go deeper — into the data architecture, the business logic, and the downstream dependencies — so the output holds up as the business changes.

A typical engagement: we map how your team currently gets from raw data to finished report. We identify every manual step, every brittle formula, every system that doesn't talk to another. Then we rebuild it — automated data extraction, a master model that refreshes on its own, and outputs that distribute on a schedule.

Your team stops building. They start reviewing.

On AI — how it works, and why you can trust it.

We don't let AI calculate anything. Python scripts handle every extraction, transformation, and calculation — the same methodology your team uses manually, automated in deterministic code. The AI layer sits on top: it reads the validated numbers and writes the commentary. The narrative, the anomaly flags, the board-ready insights.

Scripts ensure the numbers are right. AI ensures leadership understands what they mean.

This is enterprise-grade integration. Not a chatbot approximating your revenue.

The client retired a major division and reallocated product lines across four new business units. Every formula, every external reference, every roll-up needed rebuilding. One broken link would cascade errors across the entire reporting chain.

We rebuilt the full P&L and franchise sales reporting from the ground up — new BU hierarchy, automated data extraction, dynamic models feeding downstream reporting, board-ready dashboards for leadership.

CRM-to-Finance Intelligence

PE-backed AI consultancy · 200+ specialists · $45M+ pipeline

CRM data in HubSpot, delivery data in timesheets, actuals in spreadsheets — nobody connecting them. The weekly pipeline report was being built manually every week: log in, pull data, reconcile, write commentary, email leadership. Hours of work for a single report.

We built automated HubSpot extraction via API, Python data aggregation scripts, an LLM narrative layer on validated figures, automated pipeline emails, 13-week cashflow forecast, invoicing automation, and employee-level profitability analysis.

→ 1 full week saved per monthly close cycle

→ 2 days of recurring weekly manual work eliminated

→ Pipeline, delivery, and actuals connected in one view for the first time

Board Pack & Cashflow Automation

National staffing group · 300+ contractors · Multi-entity structure

Five entities, three ERPs, and a finance team producing the consolidated board pack in Excel every month. The process took four days, involved manual inter-company eliminations, and broke every time someone changed a tab name. Leadership had no rolling forecast — just last month's actuals and gut feel.

We built a centralised data model pulling from all three systems, automated the consolidation and elimination logic, and layered a 13-week rolling cashflow forecast on top. The board pack now generates in under an hour.

→ Board pack turnaround reduced from 4 days to under 1 hour

→ 13-week rolling cashflow forecast delivered for the first time

→ Three separate ERPs consolidated into a single reporting view

→ Month-end close shortened by 2 full days

→ Finance team now spends month-end on analysis, not assembly
